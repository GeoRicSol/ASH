Lawn Care

Essential lawn care involves regular mowing (weekly/fortnightly, March–October).

Key maintenance tasks involves watering, feeding with fertilizer, removing weeds and addressing waterlogged soil.

Seasonal maintenance tasks involves treatments to remove matting (thatch) material.

Key Lawn Care Practices

Mowing: During peak growth, mow once a week, but avoid cutting too low to prevent damage. We regularly service all our equipment to maintain sharp mower blades to avoid tearing grass.

Watering: We recommend to water deeply every 7–10 days rather than light daily sprinkling to encourage deep roots.

Aeration: We use a garden fork to spike the lawn throughout the season, which helps air, water, and nutrients reach the roots and alleviates compaction.

Scarification: Remove moss and thatch (dead grass) with a rake to promote healthy growth.

Feeding:We recommend using lawn fertilizer to promote strong, thick, green grass.

Repairing:Damaged and bare areas can be treated by spreading patch repair mix over effected areas.
